# Solutions

You're free to look up solutions or copy code from somewhere else.

You could copy and paste every line of code from another source and submit it as your own.

No one is going to stop you.

However, that completely misses the purpose of these challenges.

The goal isn't simply to finish a task or earn points. It's to become a better programmer.

Every challenge is an opportunity to practice:

* Writing code
* Problem solving
* Critical thinking
* Debugging
* Researching documentation
* Improving your programming speed and confidence

That's why each challenge lasts an entire month.

That's why submissions are still accepted after the deadline.

And that's why your score isn't based solely on completing the challenge. It's also based on your effort and your ability to explain your own solution.
When you truly understand something, you're usually able to explain it clearly enough that someone else could learn from it.

Everyone learns at a different pace, and that's perfectly okay.

Some participants may finish in a few hours. Others may need several weeks. Both are valid, as long as you're learning along the way.

Keep in mind that using someone else's completed solution will likely teach you very little. Struggling with a problem, making mistakes, and finding your own way forward are all valuable parts of the learning process.

If you get stuck, don't give up.

Ask questions. Reach out to the moderators. Use the **#ask-for-help** channel. We're here to guide you, not to solve the challenge for you, but to help you keep moving forward.

No matter how difficult a challenge feels or how long it takes you to complete it, if you finish having learned something new, then your time was well spent.

Good luck in your journey.
